WWDC 2026: The Good, the Ugly and the Preposterous

08-06-2026
By Sofia Maldonado

The WWDC 2026 keynote just wrapped up. It was slightly shorter than I thought it would be and I found it bizarre that they didn't really go into any of the specific platforms individually. To be fair, Apple OSs are more interconnected and similar than ever so I guess that makes sense.

This year they focused on 3 things: Platform Improvements, Child Safety and of course the new Siri.

Platform Improvements

This was honestly the one I was most exicted for. As I said in my may overview last month, I expected Apple to at least moderate some of Liquid Glass's most egregious design sins. They made apps on MacOS have consistent corners and sidebars don't "float" anymore, something that I personally found extremely annoying on Apple Music on iPad. And best of all, there is now a system-wide slider to change how clear or opaque Liquid Glass appears, which is SOOO appreciated. I am still not a fan of everything floating around everywhere (for example, on WhatsApp, and I don't think that will change like with the sidebars), but I may change my mind if I see it with more opacity.

I am also very excited for the claimed performance improvements. iPadOS 26 felt like a handbrake on my 9th-gen iPad (in great part because of the Liquid Glass, part of why I hate it so much), so I really hope performance gets better. Also, all devices that got iOS 26 will also get iOS 27, the same for iPadOS.

Child Safety

This is the one I will talk about least because these features don't apply to me, as I'm neither a child nor a parent. There's a spectrum with child safety features, as they are obviously valuable and useful tools to deter bad actors, of which there are many on Apple devices, but there are definitely bad parents out there whose kids will now be less free on their own devices due to some of the restrictions on apps and messages. With that said, the feature that can notify parents if nude content either tries to enter the device or (even worse) leave the device is a great feature. Kind of conflicted overall though, definitely glad these features didn't exist when I was a kid.

But also, the section where they panned out to "unsafe" apps for kids was hilarious, should have included Roblox you cowards!!!

Siri + Apple Intelligence

The big one. Something I really appreciated right off the bat was Craig Federighi criticizing his industry's excessive need to shove AI everywhere just for the sake of AI. Great message that felt slightly undermined by what happened later, but we will get there.

I have kind of memory-holed the WWDC 2024 Keynote but I do believe that the Apple Intelligence features presented today are a lot more capable than the ones that were announced back then. Something I noticed while watching was that one of the presenters actually used the word agentic to describe a feature at one point, a term that didn't really even exist to describe AI systems until late 2024. A testament to how fast the AI ecosystem is moving. Many features here, like natural language shortcuts are very impressive, and the new Siri UI and app are both extremely pretty.

Unfortunately, I now need to get critical, beginning with Image Playground. I am a firm believer that image generation and manipulation with AI is boring and unimpressive at best and creepy and dangerous at worst, and not even Apple can shake that belief for me. I am genuinely curious about the guardrails they have in place for this stuff, and I wonder how long it will take for people to bypass them.

Some of those cool features, like the reminder demos and message demos, seem to be pretty closed off to the ecosystem, which is great if you mostly use Apple services and devices, but if you're not very locked in or, like me, live in a country where those aren't real options (I have never realistically used iMessage) these are nothingburgers. I didn't catch any APIs or kits to implement these very directly with other apps, so it may just be limited to those aforementioned new shortcuts which sucks. Even to people in the United States, it feels like punishment for being less into the ecosystem with less features. Very whack!

However, nothing for me is as big of an offender as the compatibility lists. I got dazy trying to keep count of the different compatiblity lists for these features, and even on Apple's own website it isn't really clear what stuff is compatible on which devices. "Apple Intelligence" as a brand of features is still only available on phones newer than the iPhone 15 Pro. bad (not Bad, bad) news for me, an iPhone 14 Pro user. However, considering these other features are dependent on private cloud compute, it makes absolutely no sense that any person with at least the latest 27-numbered version of their OS couldn't use them.

Worse of all though, the Apple Intelligence features that require on-device compute will only be available on the iPhone 17 Pro, Pro Max and iPhone Air, the M4 or later iPads, M3 or later Macs and the 2nd gen Apple Vision Pro. If you spent $3500 dollars to buy a 1st-gen Apple Vision Pro, or $2000+ to buy an M2 Max Macbook Pro, or $1000+ for an iPhone 16 Pro in 2024 after seeing that year's WWDC keynote announcing these features originally, Apple doesn't think you deserve their latest and greatest AI features. That's a dick punch, one of the worst things I've seen them do recently.

And to top it all off, the private cloud compute features are limited in how much you can use them, and more access will only be available to iCloud+ subscribers. Absolutely awful.

Apple is obviously to blame for all of the worst things I've mentioned here, but it also speaks to how pervasive and omnipresent AI is that companies, even great technology companies like Apple, can succumb to such awful practices that are bad for consumers just because a small group of people are burning trillions of dollars to convince people that AI is good for them. I believe that most consumer applications for AI are completely unnecessary and only exist so that these companies and very powerful men can justify their money burning by making ordinary people's lives worse.

General Keynote Stuff

I end this with some general observations I made watching the keynote.
- Compared to other keynotes where Apple Campus is totally empty, this time there were a lot of people walking around, even behind and to the side of the presenters. I really liked this detail and I hope they continue to do this in their pre-recorded presentations. I do wonder if that had an influence on the plentiful leaks...
- Of course they mentioned the Artemis II iPhone photos.
- They changed the battery icon
- The Device Hub feature where you can stretch an app view horizontally is so obviously a preview for developers for when the iPhone Fold releases later this year, that was so funny.
- Finally, no John Ternus! At all! Speaking of which...

I really appreciated Tim Cook getting to wear his Pride Band and giving his final farewell in his last Keynote as CEO. Thanks Tim, I really hope John Ternus has more of a spine to put Craig Federighi back in line for the dumb bullshit he makes once in a while.
