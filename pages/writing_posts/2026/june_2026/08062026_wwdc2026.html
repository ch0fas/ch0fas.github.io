WWDC 2026

08-06-2026
By Sofia Maldonado

The WWDC 2026 keynote just wrapped up. It was slightly shorter than I thought it would be and I found it bizarre that they didn't really go into any of the specific platforms individually. To be fair, Apple's OS's are more interconnected and similar than ever so I guess that makes sense.

This year they focused on 3 things: Platform Improvements, Child Safety and of course the new Siri.

Platform Improvements

This was honestly the one I was most exicted for. As I said in my may overview last month, I expected Apple to begin to at least moderate some of Liquid Ass's most egregious design sins. They made apps on MacOS have consistent corners and sidebars don't "float" anymore, something that I personally found extremely annoying on Apple Music on iPad. And best of all, there is now a system-wide slider to change how clear or opaque Liquid Glass appears, which is SOOO appreciated. I am still not a fan of everything floating around everywhere (for example, on WhatsApp, and I don't think that will change like with the sidebars), but I may change my mind if I see it with more opacity.

I am also very excited for the claimed performance improvements. iPadOS 26 felt like a handbrake on my 9th-gen iPad (in great part because of the Liquid Glass, part of why I hate it so much), so I really hope performance gets better. Also, all devices that got iOS 26 will also get iOS 27 and I hope that is also the case with the iPads. My iPad has the same chip as the iPhone 11 that will also get iOS 27 so I doubt it won't get the update.

Child Safety

This is the one I will talk about least because these features don't apply to me, either as a child nor a parent. There is also a spectrum with child safety features, as they are obviously valuable and useful tools to deter bad actors, of which there are many on Apple devices. Also, there are definitely bad parents out there whose kids will now be less free on their own devices due to some of the restrictions on apps and messages. They did mention they are bringing APIs for these features to all developers, so I expect that it will be able to be implemented on non-Apple apps like WhatsApp and Discord, even if it takes a little while. Kind of conflicted overall, definitely glad these features didn't exist when I was a kid.

Siri + Apple Intelligence

The big one. This was what most people were talking about going into the week of course. Something I really appreciated right off the bat was Craig Federighi criticizing his industry's excessive need to shove AI everywhere just for the sake of AI. It insists upon itself. He repeated one of Apple's core principles: they will probably never "invent" a new technology, but they will be the ones to implement it in a way that makes it work best for the consumer, which I do believe is the case.

I have kind of memory-holed the WWDC 2024 Keynote but I do believe that the Apple Intelligence features presented today are a lot more capable than the ones that were announced back then. Something I noticed while watching was that one of the presenters actually used the word agentic to describe a feature at one point, a term that didn't really even exist to describe AI systems until late 2024. A testament to how fast the AI ecosystem is moving. Many features here, like natural language shortcuts are very impressive, and the new Siri UI and app are both extremely pretty.

Unfortunately, I now need to get critical, beginning with Image Playground. I am a firm believer that image generation and manipulation with AI is boring and unimpressive at best and creepy and dangerous at worst, and not even Apple can shake this belief. I am genuinely curious about the Guardrails they have in place for this stuff, and I wonder how long it will take for people to bypass them.

Some of those cool features, like the reminder demos and message demos, seem to be pretty closed off to the ecosystem, which is great if you mostly use Apple services and devices, but if you're not very locked in or, like me, live in a country where those options aren't real options (I have never realistically used iMessage). I didn't catch any APIs or kits to implement these very directly with other apps, it may be limited to those aforementioned new shortcuts which sucks. Even to people in the United States, it feels like punishing people for being less into the ecosystem with less features. Very whack!

However, nothing for me is as big of an offender as the compatibility lists. I got dazy trying to keep count of the different compatiblity lists for these features, and even on Apple's own website it isn't really clear what stuff is compatible on which devices. "Apple Intelligence" as a brand of features is still only available on phones older than the iPhone 15 Pro. bad (not Bad, bad) news for me, an iPhone 14 Pro user.
