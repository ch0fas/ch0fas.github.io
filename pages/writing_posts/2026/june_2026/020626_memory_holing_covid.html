Memory-Holing COVID

02-06-2026
By Sofia Maldonado

Last month I was watching Snazzy Labs' retrospective on Tim Cook's tenure as Apple's CEO. It was a very good video that I recommend people see, but I kept thinking about a particular line in the video. When talking about Face ID, specifically its ineffectiveness during the time of COVID, he says:

"They finally end up shipping mask support within Face ID after COVID's basically already over[...] the end of 2021."

This stuck out to me because that was most definitely not the case. The Omicron variant (remember the variants?) started to spread in late 2021 and in very early 2022 (when the feature launched) the WHO was reporting 15M+ new cases per week. Thankfully the vaccines had reduced the mortality rate significantly by this point, but tens of thousands were still dying weekly and hospitals were still filling up. In my mind, COVID didn't really truly "end" until late 2022, but it is interesting how quickly we have memory-holed some of the tougher stretches of the pandemic. When people talk about COVID they mostly refer to what happened in 2020 but it was a multi-year thing.

Very good video regardless! I really enjoyed it but I am shocked by how humanity has decided to collectively forget about the worst effects of the pandemic. I really hope it is the last in my lifetime.
