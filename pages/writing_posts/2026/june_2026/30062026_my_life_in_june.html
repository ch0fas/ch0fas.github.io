My Life in June 2026

30-06-2026
By Sofia Maldonado

June was one of the busier months I've had in a while, and is very deserving of a big overview.

In Memoriam: Marjane Satrapi

One of the sadder stories that came out of this month was the tragic and early passing of Marjane Satrapi, an Iranian illustrator and author best known for her Persepolis graphic novels abbout growing up as a girl in post-revolution Iran. I am saddened that she was never able to see the people of Iran freed of both domestic and foreign oppression. nunquam obliviscar

Java

As I said in my last month overview, I've been taking an Object Oriented Programming class this summer where I've been learning Java. I was already familiar with the object oriented paradigm because a lot of data science work deals with objects (either DataFrames, or models that have their own constructors and attributes), but this month I've learned more of the theory of inheritance, polymorphism, generalization vs specialization and more. I've reflected a lot on the ways in which I wrote software in the past and will probably use more object oriented programming for future projects.

But more crazily, this month something happened that I never thought would: I fell in love with Java. It is such an elegant language (even if slightly verbose), but so much of it just makes sense. It is simple to manage and extend bigger projects, and I find it so easy to write and read what someone else has made. Object Oriented design also feels so much worse to implement in other lagnuages (but this is probably just me being accustomed to the paradigm with Java's tooling because it is what I am using to learn). Me knowing Java also means that I will soon be able to make Minecraft mods which I am genuinely very excited for, it is a childhood dream.

Knicks in ✋🏻

The NBA Finals happened this month. I consider myself a basketball fan (even if I don't consider myself a fan of any particular team). As a fan with no stakes in the series, these finals were some of the greatest basketball I've ever seen. I am so happy for both the Knicks and New York and I hope next season also goes very well for both them and the Spurs.

WWDC

WWDC happened this month. I won't mention it much here because I already wrote an entire article with my impressions, but somethiing I will give Apple credit for is that, so far, the performance improvements have been legit. I installed the Developer Beta on my 9th-gen iPad the day it came out and have been pretty impressed so far by how much quicker apps launch and work compared to iPadOS 26. The battery life has been a lot worse though, but this is a symptom of all beta software and it will get better come September.

Breath Of The Wild

This month I re-played Breath Of The Wild. I first played it last year and instantly felt like it was one of the best games I had ever played. Now, after going through it again, I am absolutely sure that it is the best game I have ever played.

I cannot fathom how much love and effort went into making this game as close to perfection as it is. The art style is still beautiful. The soundtrack is dreamy. The game's mechanics are so ridiculously well made I am shocked that I haven't found a single bug in my two playthroughs. The story is heartbreaking and (almost) all quests are extremely rewarding and fun. Breath Of The Wild is the greates expression so far of what the medium of video games can achieve, and it is an absolute triumph.

Blondies

This month I finished reading Gentlemen Prefer Blondes by Anita Loos. I wasn't particularly fascinated by it, probably because it is a century-old comedy so many of the jokes fly over my head (not all though, the book has some gems that any modern woman can appreciate), but it was such a short read that I don't really regret it.

The Most Beautiful Song

After finishing Gentlemen Prefer Blondes I began a novel that has been on my radar for years: The Song of Achilles. I've never read either The Iliad or The Odyssey because they're nearly 3000 years old and even more mdoern translations don't really work for me, but I've always been very interested in both, but especially The Iliad. The Song of Achilles is the story of the Trojan War as told from the perspective of Patroclus, mainly focusing on his relationship with Achilles. As of writing I haven't yet finished the book but it is one of the most beautifully written novels I've ever read. It expresses yearning and romantic and sexual desire so flawlessly I am left warm inside and outside after every reading session.

Movies

This month I only saw two movies. The World Cup definitely took up a lot of my time on the evenings that I would usually devote to film.

The first was Medianeras, or Sidewalls as it's known in English. One of the sweetest movies I've ever seen that highlights just how magical it is that we know and coincide with the people we know and share life with. And it has what may be my favorite ending to any movie ever. I cannot recommend this movie enough.

The second was Museo, a movie based on the real 1985 heist in Mexico City's Museo Nacional de Antropología e Historia (National Museum of Anthropology and History), where two veterinary students stole invaluable indigenous pieces of art, some of which have never been recovered. I didn't like it as much as I thought I would and haven't thought much of it since watching it but it also wasn't bad.

Music

I only listened to two albums in June, also again because of the World Cup.

The first was Fetch The Bold Cutters by Fiona Apple. I've already listened to both Tidal and When The Pawn... by her, and she is a phenomenal songwriter that is even better at curating incredible production.FTBC doesn't quite reach the same heights as When The Pawn..., one of my favorite albums of all time, but I would still recommend it obviously, all of her music is worth listening to.

The secon was You Seeem Pretty Sad for a Girl So In Love, by Olivia Rodrigo. I was really anticipating this album as was evident last month and it didn't really dissapoint. I like the concept of the album haivng two different halves because the songs in the middle blend them together very well. I am currently in a love-loving mood so I was mostly drawn to the first half of the album (drop dead, stupid song, u + me = <3 and especially honeybee which was definitely my favorite), but the cure, begged, less and cigarette smoke were also great.

Last month I also said that I was really anticipating listening to the new Strokes album. That was until they delayed it 4 weeks on June 11th. What could be even worse than that? Not knowing about the delay because June 11th was World Cup Inauguration Day and only finding out until the day that it was originally slated to come out.

The thing that I am anticipating the most in July is probably the release of The Odyssey by Christopher Nolan. He has been widely criticized by some history nerds for some of the costume and design decisions for this movie, but if Oppenheimer taught me anything is that Nolan is excellent at creating historical fiction. I expect this movie to also be excellent.

Unfortunately, as I stated above, I am still waiting on The Strokes to release their new album, so I guess that's something more that I can add to the calendar.
